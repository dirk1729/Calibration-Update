\begin{frame}{\ttbar Calibration Strategy}
    \centering
    Following the strategy of the previous calibrations detailed in  \href{https://cds.cern.ch/record/2774902/}{\beamergotobutton{R21 Int Note}}. Semi-leptonic \ttbar events are selecting using a tag and probe method; This requires a $t\rightarrow Wb \rightarrow \ell\nu + \text{b-jet}$ as the tag-side and $t\rightarrow Wb \rightarrow q\bar{q} b$ as the probe-side.
    \includegraphics[width=0.70\textwidth]{img/tagandprobe1.png}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Objects \& Selections}
\centering
All selections are made in \href{https://gitlab.cern.ch/dallen/easyjet/-/tree/calibration?ref_type=heads}{\beamergotobutton{easyjet}}
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{img/obj_defs.png}
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{img/obj_selection.png}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Process}
    All selections are made in easyjet with the "xbbcalib-ntupler". The mc samples are then processed further with "XbbCalibPostProcess.py" to add event weights. From there the normfactors and scalefactors can be extracted by performing a maximum likelihood fit TRExFitter.
    \[
    \begin{gathered}
N_{\text {tag }}^{\text {data }}=\mu * S F * N_{\text {tag }}^{\text {ttbar }}+N_{\text {tag }}^{\text {other }} \\
N_{\text {untag }}^{\text {data }}=\mu * \frac{1-\epsilon^{\mathrm{MC}} * S F}{1-\epsilon^{\mathrm{MC}}} * N_{\text {untag }}^{\text {ttbar }}+N_{\text {untag }}^{\text {other }}
\end{gathered}
    \]
    
\end{frame}
